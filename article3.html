Google Loon Project

“The Internet is becoming the town square for the global village of tomorrow.” Bill Gates. This is a perfectly reasonable statement considering the Internet in a little more than ten years has evolved into such a growing force in the global society. Inspiring revolutions through social networks, spearheading a whole new bits economy, and heuristically transforming education it is hard to imagine life without our new virtual megacity. It is hard to fathom life without a surplus of information in our pocket. Living in a first world country like myself creates an false allusion that the internet it is consistently uniting all cultures and breaking the economic information barrier for all people. However According to the international telecommunications union roughly 61% of the world population currently does not have internet access; a staggering statistic considering that for many first world citizens it remains the primary source for communication, research and entertainment. In more than 2/3 of southern hemisphere countries connection costs exceed a month of income. A complex network and static infrastructure of fiber optic cables crisscrossing the globe has crippled innovation for what has become an exponentially growing service. Needless to stay, the world as a whole is being constrained to poor Internet infrastructure and lack of innovation for an extremely powerful service, particularly for developing countries. Internet service itself has accounted for 21% of GDP growth yet for the direst families it remains inaccessible. The complicated infrastructure of Internet connection calls for extreme measures and outside the box. Fortunately there is a studio in Mountain View California that specializes in purposely attempting moonshot ideas. GoogleX, a surreptitious lab founded in 2010, has since set about to attempt 100 high-risk high reward moonshots in technology. Google Loon, a distinctive and public moonshot, was spearheaded by Google in 2011. Counteracting traditional Internet connection via the earth and instead going a little more scfi. Although this Google’s latest efforts prove that it could potentially surprisingly work and ultimately connect third world countries eager for its resources. Using wid patterns and millions of ballons google hopes it can create a whole new wifi infranstructure. Unlike airplanes which stay around 10 kilometers under the atmosphere the ballons launch to 20 kilometers above the atmosphere into the stratosphere. This way the ballons can catch altering wind currents by vertically moving up and down and ensuring an entire area is connected. First an initial balloon connects to a high powered base station 40 kilometers below. This ballon then distributes its wifi through its atenae to suroounding ballons within close proximity creating a ripple effect that eventually canvases the entire earth. Using a somehwt futuristic lloking antennae inhabitants can then connect through a local antennae connected to their PC. A key factor when planning such a grandiose undertcking was the consistency at which the ballons gave wifif to inhabitants on earth. In order to regulate the flow of ballons google team cteated a complex mission control which can control the ballons height and which wind current to take using adat from the the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration creating algorithms that can control the balloons location and replace ballons when they have moved to another area. The which looks like a miniature red ballon itself consists of a a green patch antenna which recives signals directly or reflects of a round 2d reflector depending on the ballons position in the sky and what angle the signals are ercieved at. Unlike a traditional satalite dish which can stay fixated at a certain angle because satalites remain in corresponding motion with the earth The balloons themselves use a thin 3 mil thick superpressured pastic which inflates similarly to mylar party balloons creating a more durable structure resitent to high atmospheric pressures.
